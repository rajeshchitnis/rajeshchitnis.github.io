My Research Interests

  • Designing efficient algorithms for NP-hard (graph) problems
  • Compact summaries for massive datasets via streaming algorithms and kernels
  • (Tight) Lower bounds for algorithms via complexity theory

Ideas for Student Projects

  1. Understand the theoretical definitions of important real-world problems. Then design simple heuristics for these (typically) hard problems and see how they perform on real-world datasets.
    Some examples of such problems are
      k-Center: The simple greedy algorithm gives 2-approximation. How does this work on real-world (or random) datasets?
      Gerrymandering: This paper discusses a greedy algorithm
      Vertex Cover: The simple greedy algorithm gives 2-approximation. How does this work on real-world data sets?

  2. Build your own SAT solver
    You can test performance of your SAT solver on standard datasets, and use it to try to solve real-world problems like UoB timetabling.

  3. Implement known (stable) matching algorithms
    You will learn about these algorithms, implement and use them for real-world problems like allocating students to project supervisors. We've actually used one such algorithm for the allocation this year!

  4. Visualization of algorithms streaming algorithms on large datasets
    You will learn about how to implement known algorithms (graphs, sorting, etc.) and then how to visualize them. Here is one example of such a project.

  5. Implement known streaming algorithms on large datasets
    You will learn about streaming algorithms, and how to implement them on large datasets

  6. Difficult: Design (and implement) new, improved algorithms
    This projects will be theoretical, and you must have strong background in algorithms/mathematics/complexity theory for these types of projects.

  7. Ideas suggested by students
    I am happy to consider other topics, as long as they are related to algorithms.
