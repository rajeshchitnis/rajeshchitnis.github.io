My Research Interests

  • Designing efficient algorithms for NP-hard (graph) problems
  • Compact summaries for massive datasets via streaming algorithms and kernels
  • (Tight) Lower bounds for algorithms via complexity theory


Ideas for Student Projects

  1. Build your own SAT solver
    You can test performance of your SAT solver on standard datasets, and use it to try to solve real-world problems like UoB timetabling.
  2. Implement known (stable) matching algorithms
    You will learn about these algorithms, implement and use them for real-world problems like allocating students to project supervisors. We've actually used one such algorithm for the allocation this year!
  3. Implement known streaming algorithms on large datasets
    You will learn about streaming algorithms, and how to implement them on large datasets
  4. Difficult: Design (and implement) new, improved algorithms
    This projects will be theoretical, and you must have strong background in algorithms/mathematics/complexity theory for these types of projects.
  5. Ideas suggested by students
    I am happy to consider other topics, as long as they are related to algorithms.
